About Me

My name is Robert Weaver and I am a recent graduate from the University of Puget Sound.

I was born in Seattle back in 1999 and grew up on Mercer Island after moving when I was around the age of 2. I attended the Mercer Island public schools and graduated from Mercer Island High School in 2018. In high school I participated in jv and varsity baseball and basketball. In baseball I played all four of my high school years, playing on the varsity team from my sophomore through senior years, making the state playoffs all three years (2016-2018), being named an all state pitcher two years in a row (2017-2018) and being named Player of the week from the Washington Interscholastic Athletics Association.

After highschool I came the University of Puget Sound. I came to the school as a part of the varsity baseball team and a exercise science major. The spring of my freshman year I went through the rush process and accepted my bid from Phi Delta Theta Fraternity becoming an active member and later an elected member of the chapter's executive team. My sophomore year I switched majors twice finally settling on computer science and not looking back since.

Currently, I am seeking work as a full-stack or back-end software engineer.
