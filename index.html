About Me

My name is Robert Weaver and I am currently in my senior year at the University of Puget Sound.

I was born in Seattle back in 1999 and grew up on Mercer Island after moving when I was around the age of 2. I attended the Mercer Island public schools and graduated from Mercer Island High School in 2018. In high school I participated in athletics, I played jv and varsity baseball and basketball. In baseball I played all four of my high school years, playing on the varsity team from my sophomore through senior years, making the state playoffs all three years, being named an all state pitcher two years in a row and being named Player of the week from the Washington Interscholastic Athletics Association.

After highschool I came the University of Puget Sound, where I currently am today. I came to the school as a part of the varsity baseball team and a exercise science major. The spring of my freshman year I went through the rush process and accepted my bid from Phi Delta Theta becoming an active member at the end of the school year. My sophomore year I switched majors twice finally settling on computer science and not looking back once since. Additionally I ran for an exec position in the fraternity and won becoming the chapter's brotherhood chair. My junior year I did not do much of anything eventful, covid prevented me from doing much besides attend my online classes. Now I am excited as I watch things start to adapt and move back towards a better tomorrow.
